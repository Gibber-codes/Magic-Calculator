# Magic: The Gathering Turn Structure - Quick Reference

## Beginning Phase

### 1. Untap Step
- Untap all permanents you control (no priority)

### 2. Upkeep Step
- Triggered abilities that occur "at the beginning of upkeep" trigger
- Players can cast instants/activate abilities

### 3. Draw Step
- Active player draws a card
- Players can cast instants/activate abilities

---

## Pre-Combat Main Phase
- Play one land (if you haven't already this turn)
- Cast spells (sorceries, creatures, artifacts, enchantments, planeswalkers)
- Activate abilities

---

## Combat Phase

### 1. Beginning of Combat
- Triggered abilities that occur "at beginning of combat" trigger
- Last chance to cast spells before attackers are declared

### 2. Declare Attackers Step
- Active player declares which creatures are attacking
- "When attacks" triggers occur
- Players can cast instants/activate abilities

### 3. Declare Blockers Step
- Defending player declares which creatures are blocking
- "When blocks" triggers occur
- Players can cast instants/activate abilities

### 4. Combat Damage Step
- Creatures deal damage simultaneously (unless first/double strike)
- Players can cast instants/activate abilities after damage

### 5. End of Combat Step
- "At end of combat" triggers occur
- Last chance to affect combat creatures

---

## Post-Combat Main Phase
- Play one land (if you haven't already this turn)
- Cast spells (sorceries, creatures, artifacts, enchantments, planeswalkers)
- Activate abilities

---

## Ending Phase

### 1. End Step
- "At the beginning of the end step" triggers occur
- Players can cast instants/activate activities

### 2. Cleanup Step
- Active player discards down to maximum hand size (usually 7)
- Damage is removed from creatures
- "Until end of turn" effects end
- (Usually no priority unless triggered abilities occur)

---

## Pro Tips for Your IDE
- Players receive priority in each step (except Untap and Cleanup)
- The active player always receives priority first
- You can only cast sorcery-speed spells during your main phases when the stack is empty
